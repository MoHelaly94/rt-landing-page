Review it today, and discuss it tomorrow

    Initial presentation and assessment
  • 40 years old male, conscious and communicating Patient.
  • Admitted to ICU with complain of chest pain, and nocturnal dyspnea, especially when lying on right side.
  • Patient has no past medical history.
  • Vitals shows: RR 25 breath/m, HR 100 beat/m, SpO2 89% and Temperature 38 C
  • By auscultation: right anterior basal and posterior mid- basal inspiratory crackles.
    Recommended diagnostics (baseline guide)
  • Lung imaging (e.g CXR, ultrasound): to identify the underlying cause.
  • ABG: to assess baseline oxygenation, ventilation, and acid-base status.
